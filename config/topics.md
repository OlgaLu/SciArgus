#Systematic Conservation Planning in Marine Environments 
Research on the application of systematic conservation planning frameworks and decision-support tools — particularly Marxan and related algorithms — to marine and coastal environments. This includes the identification of conservation priorities, the design of reserve networks, the use of spatial prioritisation methods, scenario analysis and trade-off assessment between conservation and human uses. Of particular interest are applications in the Northeast Atlantic and European waters, and studies that integrate ecological, socioeconomic, and governance data into planning workflows. 

#Marine Protected Areas Design and Effectiveness 
Studies on the design, implementation, management, and effectiveness of Marine Protected Areas (MPAs) and MPA networks, including fully and highly protected areas, no-take zones, and area-based conservation measures under the 30x30 biodiversity targets. This encompasses MPA monitoring and evaluation, social and ecological outcomes, connectivity, spillover effects, blue carbon, and the political economy of MPA establishment. Relevant policy contexts include the EU Marine Strategy Framework Directive, the EU Biodiversity Strategy, and the Kunming-Montreal Global Biodiversity Framework.

#Cumulative Impacts and Anthropogenic Pressures on Marine Ecosystems 
Research on the assessment, mapping, and modelling of cumulative human impacts on marine ecosystems, including methodological advances in pressure mapping, impact scoring, and the integration of multiple stressors (fishing, pollution, climate change, shipping, energy infrastructure). Studies that develop or apply cumulative impact frameworks — such as Halpern-style approaches — in European or Northeast Atlantic seas, or that link pressure assessments to conservation decision-making and policy advice, are of particular interest.

#Deep-sea Ecosystems and Vulnerable Marine Ecosystems 
Research on the ecology, distribution, conservation, and management of deep-sea habitats and Vulnerable Marine Ecosystems (VMEs), including cold-water corals, sponge aggregations, and other benthic communities. This covers ROV and autonomous survey methods, species distribution modelling for deep-sea taxa, the threats posed by bottom fishing and other human activities, and the governance frameworks — including ICES advice and OSPAR designations — aimed at protecting these habitats in the North Atlantic and European waters.

#Small Pelagic Fish and Fisheries Management 
Research on the ecology, population dynamics, and management of small pelagic species — particularly anchovy (Engraulis encrasicolus) and sardine (Sardina pilchardus) — in European and Atlantic waters. Topics of interest include stock assessment methods, survey design and uncertainty, environmental drivers of recruitment and distribution shifts, ecosystem-based fisheries management, and the interface between fisheries science, the Common Fisheries Policy (CFP), and broader conservation objectives.

#Mrine megafauna Conservation in the Northeast Atlantic 
Research on the ecology, distribution, population status, and conservation of seabirds, marine mammals (including cetaceans and pinnipeds), sharks, rays, and chimaeras (chondrichthyes) in the Northeast Atlantic, Mediterranean, and adjacent seas. Topics of particular interest include bycatch and entanglement, underwater noise impacts, distribution modelling, protected area use, cumulative impact assessment, and the policy frameworks governing marine megafauna protection under the EU Habitats Directive, OSPAR, and the BBNJ Agreement.

#Marine Spatial Planning and Ocean Governance 
Research on marine spatial planning (MSP) processes, tools, and outcomes, including the integration of ecological and socioeconomic data into planning frameworks, stakeholder engagement, cross-border and transboundary planning challenges, and cumulative effects in MSP. Relevant policy contexts include the EU Maritime Spatial Planning Directive, the EU Ocean Pact, and the emerging EU Ocean Act, as well as comparative studies of MSP implementation across jurisdictions. Studies linking MSP to conservation outcomes, ecosystem services, and blue economy objectives are of particular interest.

#High Seas and BBNJ Ocean Governance 
Research on the governance of areas beyond national jurisdiction (ABNJ), including the implementation of the High Seas Treaty (BBNJ Agreement), the design and establishment of high seas protected areas, environmental impact assessments in international waters, and the role of regional fisheries management organisations (RFMOs) in biodiversity conservation. Studies that assess the ecological representativeness of proposed high seas conservation measures, or that model conservation scenarios under different governance frameworks, are particularly relevant.

#Marine Biodiversity Monitoring and Ecosystem Assessment 
Research on the monitoring, assessment, and indicators of marine biodiversity and ecosystem health, including developments in eDNA, underwater imaging, ROV surveys, acoustic methods, and remote sensing as applied to biodiversity monitoring. Of interest are studies developing or validating biodiversity indicators for policy (e.g. EU MSFD Good Environmental Status descriptors), integrating multiple data streams for ecosystem assessments, and addressing uncertainty and data gaps in large-scale biodiversity monitoring programmes.
