#Marine Spatial Planning and Ocean Governance
Research on marine and maritime spatial planning (MSP) methods, processes, tools, and outcomes, including the integration of ecological and socioeconomic data into planning frameworks, stakeholder engagement, cross-border and transboundary planning challenges, and cumulative effects management in MSP. Relevant policy contexts include the EU Maritime Spatial Planning Directive, the EU Ocean Pact, and the upcoming EU Ocean Act, as well as comparative studies of MSP implementation across jurisdictions globally. Studies linking MSP to conservation outcomes, ecosystem services, nature's contributions to people (NCP), and blue economy objectives are of particular interest. Key terms: marine spatial planning, maritime spatial planning, ocean zoning, intergrated coastal zoning, seascape planning, blue economy, ecosystem-based management, EBM, planning framework, ocean governance.

#High Seas and BBNJ Ocean Governance
Research on the governance of areas beyond national jurisdiction (ABNJ), including the implementation of the High Seas Treaty (BBNJ Agreement), the design and establishment of high seas marine protected areas, environmental impact assessments (EIAs) in international waters, and interactions with regional fisheries management organisations (RFMOs), the International Seabed Authority (ISA), the International Maritime Organisation (IMO), and other international agreements and institutions. Studies assessing the ecological representativeness of proposed high seas conservation measures, or modelling conservation scenarios under different governance frameworks, are particularly relevant. Key terms: BBNJ, high seas, areas beyond national jurisdiction, high seas treaty, ABNJ, deep-sea governance, international ocean law, RFMO, ISA, IMO, global ocean treaty.

#Systematic Conservation Planning and Spatial Conservation Prioritisation
Research on the application of systematic conservation planning frameworks and decision-support tools for spatial conservation prioritisation — particularly Marxan, prioritizr, and related algorithms. This includes identification of conservation priorities and targets, design of reserve networks, spatial prioritisation for zoning, scenario analysis, and trade-off assessment between conservation and human uses. Of particular interest are studies in marine, coastal, and freshwater environments, studies that account for temporal and dynamic dimensions, integrate ecological, socioeconomic, and governance data into planning workflows, or evaluate the sensitivity of planning outputs to data uncertainty. Key terms: systematic conservation planning, spatial conservation prioritisation, spatial prioritisation, Marxan, prioritizr, Zonation, reserve design, conservation targets, zoning, irreplaceability, complementarity.

#Marine Protected Areas Design and Effectiveness
Studies on the design, implementation, management, and effectiveness of Marine Protected Areas (MPAs) and MPA networks, including partially and fully or highly protected areas, no-take zones, and other effective area-based conservation measures (OECMs) under the 30x30 biodiversity targets. Topics include MPA design principles, monitoring and evaluation, social and ecological outcomes, connectivity, spillover effects, blue carbon, equity, and the political economy of MPA establishment. Relevant policy contexts include the EU Marine Strategy Framework Directive (MSFD), the EU Biodiversity Strategy, the Kunming-Montreal Global Biodiversity Framework, and the BBNJ Agreement, but studies from any region are of interest. Key terms: marine protected area, MPA, no-take zone, OECM, marine reserve, protection effectiveness, MPA network, 30x30, biodiversity targets, conservation outcomes, Kunming-Montreal, area-based conservation. 

#Cumulative Impacts and Anthropogenic Pressures on Marine Ecosystems
Research on the assessment, mapping, and modelling of cumulative human impacts on marine ecosystems, including methodological advances in pressure mapping, impact scoring, and the integration of multiple stressors such as fishing, pollution, climate change, shipping, and energy infrastructure. Studies that develop or apply cumulative impact frameworks, or that link pressure assessments to Marine Spatial Planning or conservation decision-making and policy advice, are of particular interest. Also relevant: studies on individual pressures (bottom trawling, noise, nutrient runoff, plastic pollution, offshore wind) and their ecological effects. Key terms: cumulative impacts, anthropogenic pressures, multiple stressors, impact assessment, pressure mapping, environmental impact, trawling impacts, ocean noise, marine pollution, human footprint, ecosystem vulnerability, sensitivity.

#Deep-sea Ecosystems and Vulnerable Marine Ecosystems
Research on the ecology, distribution, conservation, and management of deep-sea habitats and Vulnerable Marine Ecosystems (VMEs), including cold-water corals, sponge aggregations, seamounts, hydrothermal vents, deep-water fish, and other benthic and pelagic communities. Topics include ROV and autonomous survey methods, species distribution modelling for deep-sea taxa, threats from bottom fishing, deep-sea mining, and climate change, and governance frameworks — including the BBNJ Agreement, RFMO measures, and ISA regulations — aimed at protecting these habitats. Key terms: vulnerable marine ecosystems, VME, cold-water coral, deep-sea sponge, seamount, deep-sea mining, bottom trawling, deep-sea biodiversity, benthic habitat, species distribution model, bathyal, abyssal.

#Seabird and Marine Mammal Conservation and Management
Research on the ecology, distribution, conservation status, and management of seabirds and marine mammals, including cetaceans, pinnipeds, and seabird colonies. Topics of particular interest include bycatch and entanglement, underwater noise impacts, distribution and habitat modelling, protected area use, and cumulative impact assessment. Also relevant: studies on the policy frameworks governing their protection, including the EU Birds and Habitats Directives, OSPAR, ASCOBANS, the Convention on Migratory Species (CMS), and the BBNJ Agreement. Key terms: seabird, marine mammal, cetacean, whale, dolphin, porpoise, pinniped, bycatch, entanglement, underwater noise, ship strike, habitat model, migratory species, protected species, Habitats Directive, Birds Directive.

#Elasmobranch Conservation and Management
Research on the ecology, distribution, population status, and conservation of sharks, rays, and chimaeras (chondrichthyans), with particular interest in the Northeast Atlantic, Mediterranean, and adjacent seas, though global studies and those from other regions are also relevant. This includes species distribution modelling, bycatch and discards assessment, threat analysis and IUCN Red List assessments, the effectiveness of fisheries regulations and catch limits, protected area use by elasmobranchs, and progress toward international and EU-level elasmobranch conservation plans. Key terms: elasmobranch, shark, ray, skate, chimaera, chondrichthyes, chondrichthyan, shark conservation, bycatch, IUCN Red List, critically endangered, catch limits, shark finning, angel shark, sawfish.

#Participatory Mapping, Stakeholder Engagement, and Co-Creation in Marine Governance
Research on participatory and collaborative approaches to knowledge production and decision-making in marine and coastal governance, including participatory mapping (e.g. PPGIS, SeaSketch), public participation GIS, social–ecological systems analysis, stakeholder engagement processes in MSP and conservation planning, and co-design or co-creation frameworks. Of particular interest are studies that evaluate the quality, legitimacy, or outcomes of participation; integrate local ecological knowledge (LEK), traditional ecological knowledge (TEK), indigenous and local knowledge (ILK) into spatial planning or conservation; or examine conflicts, power dynamics, and justice in multi-stakeholder processes. Key terms: participatory mapping, PPGIS, stakeholder engagement, co-creation, co-design, public participation, SeaSketch, local ecological knowledge, LEK, traditional ecological knowledge, TEK, indigenous and local knowledge, ILK, social-ecological systems, marine governance, ocean literacy, citizen science.

#Indigenous Peoples and Local Communities in Marine Conservation and Governance
Research on the roles, rights, leadership, and lived experiences of Indigenous Peoples and Local Communities (IPLCs) in marine conservation, spatial planning, and ocean governance. Topics include Indigenous and community-led marine protected areas, free prior and informed consent (FPIC), territorial use rights in fisheries (TURFs), recognition of customary marine tenure, biocultural diversity, and the integration of Indigenous and local knowledge systems into conservation science and policy. Also of interest are studies examining equity, justice, power asymmetries, and benefit-sharing in conservation processes involving IPLCs, including within formal governance frameworks such as the Kunming-Montreal GBF (Target 22), BBNJ Agreement, and CBD. Key terms: indigenous peoples, local communities, IPLC, indigenous rights, community-based management, customary tenure, biocultural diversity, free prior informed consent, FPIC, traditional knowledge, rights-based approaches, marine tenure, ocean justice, equity, coastal communities.
